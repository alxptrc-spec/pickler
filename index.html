Keeping your data safe
Your work is saved in this browser, on this computer. Set up a backup below so you never lose it — it takes about a minute. Tap any heading to open it.
⚠️Not HIPAA compliant — use code names only

This app is not HIPAA compliant, so don't put confidential or identifying details in it — no names, initials, birth dates, or record numbers.

Use code names only, like PCH1. Everything you type is saved exactly as-is, here and in your backups.

📌Two things to get right
  • Always open it in the same browser — Chrome or Edge, not Safari. Each browser keeps its own separate copy of your data, and backups only work in Chrome and Edge.
  • Give the file a permanent home and a real name. Keep PICKLER.html in one folder you won't move (not Downloads), so you can always find it and open it the same way.
💾Back up to a file (recommended)

When you turn this on and pick a .json file, PICKLER writes a real file onto your Mac — you'll see it in Finder — and updates it automatically every time you make a change.

That file lives outside the browser, so clearing cookies or site data can't touch it. You'll then have two copies: the live one in the browser, and a real, safe backup file on your computer.

  1. Click 💾 Save ▾ at the top, then Auto-Backup.
  2. Pick or create a .json file — ideally in a synced folder (iCloud, Google Drive, Dropbox) so it's copied off your computer too.
  3. Done. If it says "Click to resume" when you reopen the app, just click it once — that's normal.

Works in Chrome and Edge. In other browsers, use Save ▾ → Export to save a copy by hand now and then.

☁️Back up to Google Drive

Saves the same backup to your Google Drive, so it's safe even if something happens to this computer.

  1. Click 💾 Save ▾, then Connect Drive, and sign in.
  2. When it says Drive: On, your data is backing up automatically.
  3. Drive only turns on when you click it — so each time you reopen the app, click Connect Drive again to switch it back on. It won't ask you to sign in on its own.

If a backup ever fails, a yellow banner appears at the top — click Reconnect to fix it.

🔒Is Google Drive safe?
  • The app only sees its own backup file. It can't open or change anything else in your Drive.
  • It goes straight to your account. The backup is sent from your browser to your own Google Drive — not to any developer's server.
  • You're in control. Disconnect any time, or remove access on your Google Account permissions page.
⚠️When you could lose your data

Your data lives in this browser. You could lose access to it if you:

  • clear this browser's cookies and site data (the option inside "Clear browsing data"),
  • open the app in a different browser, or on another computer, or
  • open it a different way than usual (for example, from a web link instead of your file).

Set up a backup above and none of this can cost you your work. A backup file on your computer (or in Google Drive) isn't affected by clearing your browser. If it ever happens, reopen PICKLER and use Save ▾ → Import to restore from your backup file.

🔗 Connect Google Drive

Paste the OAuth Client ID from your Google Cloud project. This is saved only in your browser — never sent anywhere except Google.

Don't have one yet?
You'll need to create it once in Google Cloud Console — see the setup guide for step-by-step instructions. It takes about 10 minutes.
